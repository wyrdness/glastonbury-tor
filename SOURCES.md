## Source Reliability Assessment

### Tier 1: Peer-Reviewed Academic Sources
- Archaeological reports in academic journals
- University press publications
- Scholarly editions of medieval texts

### Tier 2: Reputable Non-Academic Sources
- National Trust publications
- Historic England documentation
- Established publishers (Thames & Hudson, etc.)

### Tier 3: Alternative/Earth Mysteries Sources
- Often lack peer review
- May mix speculation with fact
- Valuable for understanding cultural beliefs
- Should be contextualized, not taken as historical fact

### Tier 4: Popular/Tourism Sources
- Often simplified or sensationalized
- May perpetuate myths without critical analysis
- Useful for understanding popular perceptions
